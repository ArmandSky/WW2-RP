Belgian Line Shattered: German Blitzkrieg Pushes Defenders to the River's Edge

Brussels, Belgium, July 1st, 1937

One month after the stunning German invasion began, the fight for Belgium has been a brutal, lopsided conflict, forcing the once-proud Belgian Army into a desperate, mass retreat. Despite fielding a massive army of 265,000 men and relying on an intricate network of fortifications, the Belgian defense has been utterly overwhelmed by the technologically superior and ruthlessly efficient German mechanized assault.

The German invasion force, estimated to be a comparatively small but highly potent 50,000 men—comprising five Infantry Divisions, two Motorized Divisions, and three Panzer Divisions—has achieved a combat ratio rarely seen in modern warfare. Reports filtering out from the shattered front lines indicate the German Wehrmacht has inflicted casualties at a staggering ratio of approximately 6-to-1 against the Belgian forces. This high kill-to-death ratio underscores the catastrophic impact of the German advantage in mobility and firepower.

The primary factor in the Belgian collapse has been the effectiveness of the German Blitzkrieg strategy. The modern tanks of the Panzer Divisions and the unrelenting, overwhelming air superiority of the Luftwaffe have rendered static Belgian fortifications largely obsolete. While the Belgian Army fought bravely, their lack of modern anti-tank weaponry and complete absence of air cover meant that their defenses were systematically bypassed, isolated, and then pulverized by coordinated strikes.

The devastating German artillery and close air support annihilated Belgian defensive lines, inflicting massive casualties and turning once-disciplined units into disorganized masses. Faced with unsustainable losses and the continuous flanking maneuvers of the German armor, the Belgian High Command has ordered a massive, strategic retreat.

The remnants of the Belgian Army are currently attempting to establish emergency defensive positions along major river lines, seeking the natural barrier of the water to slow the mechanized German advance. This desperate repositioning is a bid to buy time, regroup, and establish a coherent, if last-ditch, front. However, with German motorized units already probing for weak spots and the Luftwaffe continuing its destructive reconnaissance, the question remains whether the river lines will offer anything more than a temporary respite before the final, inevitable push. The fate of Belgium now hangs precariously on the ability of its decimated army to hold a line against a smaller but technologically fearsome foe.

Pan-Islamic Fury: Arab Uprisings Erupt as Saudi King Prepares Desert War

London, Great Britain – July 2nd 1937

Aden, British Protectorate / Persian Gulf – July 1937 : One month after Queen Mary's government launched its audacious invasion of Saudi Arabia, the gamble has ignited a devastating wave of pan-Islamic rage across the Middle East and North Africa. The invasion has achieved the unthinkable: unifying vast swathes of the Arab and Muslim world against Great Britain and its new ally, Greece.

Riots and Revolts Cripple Colonial Rule: The initial fear of widespread unrest has been realized on a catastrophic scale. Riots, demonstrations, and armed revolts are reported in virtually every British and Greek-controlled territory surrounding Saudi Arabia, stretching from Egypt and the Sudan through Iraq and the Persian Gulf, and even into newly conquered Oman and Liberia (the Greek colonial holdings). Military Bases Attacked: Crucial British military installations, particularly in Transjordan, Iraq, and the Aden Protectorate, have been subjected to direct attack by enraged local populations. Reports confirm significant structural damage to several bases, disrupting supply lines meant for the invasion force.

Equipment Stolen: Alarmingly, there are confirmed reports of military equipment being stolen during these uprisings, likely intended to arm the burgeoning resistance. While the specific types of equipment remain unknown due to the chaos, the loss suggests a rapidly escalating threat to British materiel superiority.

French Colonies Affected: The unrest has even spilled over into French colonial territories in North Africa, demonstrating the powerful religious and nationalistic solidarity sparked by the defense of Arabia. Uprisings in French colonies are straining Paris's already limited military resources.

Volunteers Flock to Saudi Arabia: Thousands of zealously motivated Muslim volunteers, driven by the call to defend the holy lands against the Western invaders, are reported to be flocking across borders and sea routes into the Saudi interior, eager to join the resistance against the British.

Saudi King Prepares the Sands Meanwhile, the military strategy of King Abdulaziz Ibn Saud has proven far more cunning than the British High Command anticipated. When British forces, after a grinding advance, finally neared the capital city, they found it largely abandoned. The expected pitched battle for the capital never materialized.

Intelligence now confirms that the King has strategically repositioned the core of his military and his loyal "sand warriors"—the experienced tribal forces—deep into the vast Arabian desert. Rather than sacrifice his army in a futile conventional defense against British artillery and air power, Ibn Saud has chosen to fight the invader on his own terms.

The King is successfully mustering a massive force of fiercely loyal tribal fighters, ready to utilize their intimate knowledge of the terrain and reliance on hit-and-run tactics to wage a protracted, debilitating guerrilla war. The British are now faced with the terrifying prospect of abandoning secure supply lines and engaging a unified, motivated, and growing resistance in the scorching, unforgiving Arabian heartland. The invasion has swiftly transformed from a swift imperial conquest into a bloody, pan-Arab insurgency.

Bolivian Civil War Erupts Amidst Border Tensions

La Paz, Bolivia – July 1937

Amidst swirling international accusations regarding unprovoked attacks on Brazilian border patrols, Bolivia has been plunged into the chaos of a full-scale civil war. Deep-seated political and regional divisions, long simmering beneath the surface, have finally boiled over, fragmenting the nation at a critical moment of external tension.

The internal conflict appears to have been triggered by a sudden and violent challenge to the central government's authority. Reports from the capital and major cities indicate fierce fighting between forces loyal to the current administration and a coalition of opposition groups, including powerful regional factions and elements within the Bolivian military itself who have defected.

The exact immediate cause of the outbreak remains unclear, but observers point to a toxic mix of factors:

Political Instability: Decades of political turbulence and frequent coups have left Bolivia's democratic institutions fragile.

Regionalism: Strong historical rivalries between the high-altitude political centers like La Paz and Sucre, and the wealthier, resource-rich lowlands have fueled secessionist tendencies.

Military Discontent: The ongoing border crisis with Brazil, and potential fallout from the alleged ambushes, may have created a pretext for dissatisfied military commanders to make their move.

The eruption of civil war has immediate and severe international consequences. The ongoing border dispute with Brazil is now vastly complicated, as the Brazilian government must decide how to handle a fractured and fighting neighbor. Furthermore, the conflict risks drawing in the powerful nations of the region, potentially destabilizing the fragile South American political equilibrium.

With the central government's control rapidly deteriorating and internal lines of communication severed, reliable information is scarce. The world watches with alarm as Bolivia, already facing external pressure, descends into a brutal internal struggle for control.

A Queen's Message

London, Great Britain – July 3rd 1937

Her Majesty Queen Mary addresses the people of Great Britain and the Colonies.

It has come to light that an attack has been made on my people. I understand that some may not like our presence, I understand that some may wish we would leave. I'm here to say it's to late. While we are not our predecessors and we do wish for mutual benefit with all our colonies.

We will not allow a single drop of British blood to fall. 36 men have lost their families, 36 families have lost their fathers, husband, sons. This will not go unnoticed. To the people of the colonies, come speak with us. We are people too, we can resolve our problems.

To the terrorists who enacted this violence, find a hole to hide in, because we are coming and I promise you someone else is going to raise your children.

A sincere ultimatum

Athens, Greece – July 3rd 1937

Citizens of the Hellenic Republic and the World. This is a message towards anyone that is listening. The Hellenic armed forces where attacked yesterday at one of our supply bases in Oman .

An investigation will be launched to find who and why this happened and the perpetrators will be prosecuted at the highest extent of the law.

Oman was promised a bright future with new jobs and an upgrade to its existing infrastructure. So as a last call i urge the people to provide us with any information they got .

May God bless our great Nation .

Kind regards,

Ioannis Metaxas
